# Chargesheet Mindmap — Quick Reference Guide

**For:** Investigating Officers (IOs)
**Module:** 2 — Chargesheet Preparation
**Version:** 1.0

---

## What Is It?

When you open a FIR in ATLAS, a **case-type-aware checklist and mindmap** appears alongside the case details. It suggests the investigation steps typically needed to build a strong, court-ready chargesheet for that particular type of offence. Think of it as an experienced colleague reminding you what to cover.

---

## How to Access

1. Log in to ATLAS and go to your **Dashboard**.
2. Open any **FIR** from your case list.
3. The **Mindmap panel** appears automatically on the right side of the screen.

---

## Key Features

| Feature | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Tree View** | A visual mindmap showing investigation branches — evidence collection, witness examination, forensics, legal provisions, and more. Expand or collapse branches as needed. |
| **Checklist View** | A flat, step-by-step list of the same items. Use this view when you prefer to work through items one by one. Toggle between views using the button at the top of the panel. |
| **Colour Coding** | **Red** = Critical / Urgent — must be addressed. **Amber** = Recommended — strongly advised for a complete chargesheet. **Green** = Optional — helpful but not essential. |
| **Status Tracking** | Mark each item as: **Open** → **In Progress** → **Addressed** / **Not Applicable** / **Disputed**. Your progress is saved automatically. |
| **Add Custom Nodes** | Add your own investigation items that are specific to your case but not covered by the generated mindmap. |
| **Export PDF** | Download a printable checklist of the mindmap with current status for offline use, meetings, or filing. |

---

## Important Notes

- **ADVISORY ONLY** — The mindmap suggests; you decide. All discretion remains with the Investigating Officer. It does not replace your professional judgement.
- Items generated by AI are marked with an **info icon** (i). Hover over it to see why the item was suggested.
- **FIR Gaps** (orange nodes) highlight information that appears to be missing from the original FIR text. Review these early.
- Every status update you make is **permanently recorded** for audit and legal transparency.
- You **cannot delete or edit** previous status entries. The system is append-only to maintain legal integrity.

---

## Tips for Daily Use

1. **Review the mindmap within 24 hours** of FIR registration to plan your investigation early.
2. **Address critical (red) items first** — these are the items most likely to weaken a chargesheet if left unattended.
3. **Use the note field** when marking an item as Not Applicable — record your reason so it is clear during review or audit.
4. **If the mindmap seems wrong** for your case type (e.g., wrong offence category), use the **"Regenerate"** option and provide a brief justification. A corrected mindmap will be generated.
5. Switch between **Tree View** and **Checklist View** depending on whether you are planning (tree) or executing (checklist).
