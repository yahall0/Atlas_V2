# Chargesheet Gap Analysis — Quick Reference Guide

**For:** Investigating Officers (IOs)
**Module:** 2 — Chargesheet Preparation
**Version:** 1.0

---

## What Is It?

When you open a chargesheet in ATLAS, the **Gap Analysis** feature checks your chargesheet against multiple sources — legal requirements, evidence standards, your investigation mindmap, and completeness rules — and shows you what may be missing or needs attention. Think of it as a second pair of eyes reviewing your chargesheet before it goes to court.

All suggestions are **advisory only**. You, the Investigating Officer, retain full responsibility and discretion over the chargesheet content.

---

## How to Access

1. Log in to ATLAS and go to your **Dashboard**.
2. Open any **Chargesheet** from your case list.
3. Click the **"Gap Analysis"** button at the top of the chargesheet view.
4. The screen splits into a **dual-pane view**: your chargesheet document on the left, and the gap analysis on the right.

---

## Understanding the Dual-Pane View

| Pane | What it shows |
|------|---------------|
| **Left pane** | Your chargesheet document — the content as it currently exists. You can scroll through and read the full chargesheet here. |
| **Right pane** | The gap analysis — a list of potential issues found by the system, sorted by severity. Each gap includes a title, explanation, and suggested remediation. |

---

## Severity Levels

Gaps are colour-coded by severity. Address them in order of importance:

| Colour | Severity | Meaning |
|--------|----------|---------|
| **Red** | **Critical** | Must fix before filing. These are issues that could lead to the chargesheet being returned by the court or cause serious problems during trial (e.g., missing accused particulars, missing charges). |
| **Orange** | **High** | Should fix. Strongly recommended for a strong, court-ready chargesheet (e.g., missing seizure memo references, evidence schedule incomplete). |
| **Yellow** | **Medium** | Review carefully. These issues may or may not apply to your specific case (e.g., investigation step planned but not reflected in chargesheet). |
| **Blue** | **Low** | For reference. Minor issues or suggestions for improvement. |
| **Grey** | **Advisory** | Informational only. These highlight best practices or optional enhancements. |

---

## Actions You Can Take

For each gap, you can take one of these actions. Your choice is permanently recorded for audit purposes.

| Action | When to use | What it means |
|--------|-------------|---------------|
| **Accept** | You agree with the gap. | You acknowledge the issue is real and plan to fix it. |
| **Modify** | You have fixed the issue. | You have already made the correction in the chargesheet. Use the note field to describe what you changed. |
| **Dismiss** | The gap does not apply to your case. | You disagree with the finding. **Always write a note** explaining why — this protects you during audit or review. |
| **Defer** | You will handle it later. | The gap is valid but you cannot address it right now (e.g., waiting for lab results, witness availability). Note your expected timeline. |
| **Escalate** | You need your supervisor's guidance. | The gap involves a legal question or policy decision above your authority. Your SHO or DYSP will be notified. |

---

## Exports

Three types of exports are available from the Gap Analysis screen:

| Export | Purpose | Who it is for |
|--------|---------|---------------|
| **Clean PDF** | A formal chargesheet document ready for court filing. Contains **no AI annotations** whatsoever — just your chargesheet content. | Court / Magistrate |
| **Review Report** | A detailed report showing all gaps, their severity, remediation steps, and your actions. Watermarked "INTERNAL REVIEW". | Your records / SHO review |
| **Redline** | Your chargesheet text with markings showing where gaps apply. Watermarked "SUPERVISOR REVIEW". | DYSP / SP review |

> **Important:** The Clean PDF is the only export suitable for court submission. Never submit the Review Report or Redline to court.

---

## Important Notes

- **ADVISORY ONLY** — The gap analysis suggests; you decide. All findings are generated by automated checks and AI models. They may contain errors. Your professional judgment takes precedence.
- Items marked with an **info icon** (i) were generated by AI and carry a disclaimer. Review these with extra care.
- Every action you take is **permanently recorded**. You cannot delete or edit previous actions. This is by design — it maintains the legal integrity of the audit trail.
- If the analysis shows "partial sources" at the top, it means some checks could not be run (e.g., evidence gap analysis was not available). You can click **"Reanalyze"** after the missing analysis is complete.

---

## Tips for Daily Use

1. **Run the gap analysis as soon as your chargesheet draft is ready** — do not wait until the filing deadline.
2. **Address critical (red) gaps first** — these are the issues most likely to cause problems in court.
3. **Always write a note when dismissing a gap** — explain why it does not apply. This protects you if the decision is questioned during review or audit.
4. **Use "Modify" after you have actually made the change** — do not mark a gap as modified until the chargesheet content is updated.
5. **Escalate when unsure** — if a gap raises a legal question you are not sure about, escalate to your supervisor rather than dismissing it.
6. **Download the Review Report for your records** — keep a copy with your case file as evidence that you reviewed the AI analysis.
